Senior Citizen Discount


Description:
Senior citizens in the Philippines receive a 20% discount on public transportation, hotel accommodations, restaurants, recreation centers, theaters, and amusement parks. They also enjoy discounts on medicines (prescription and over-the-counter) and medical and dental services in government health facilities.


Location:



Requirements:
1. 2 Recent ID Pictures
2. Prrof of age (birth certificate or government issued ID)


Steps:
1. Register with the Office for Senior Citizens Affairs (OSCA) – Visit the OSCA at the Mayor’s Office in your city or municipality.
2. Provide Required Documents – Submit two recent ID pictures and proof of age, such as a birth certificate or government-issued ID.
3. Verification Process – OSCA will verify eligibility based on age and annual income (not exceeding ₱60,000).
4. Issuance of the Senior Citizen ID – Once approved, OSCA will issue a nationally uniform Senior Citizen ID, signed by the Mayor. This ID is free of charge and must be presented to avail of discounts and privileges.
5. Replacement of Lost ID – Report lost IDs to OSCA for replacement (a minimal fee may apply).
To claim discounts, simply present your Senior Citizen ID at participating establishments, such as transport services, hotels, restaurants, pharmacies, and hospitals.

PWD Discount


Description:
PWDs in the Philippines are entitled to medical-related benefits under RA 9442, including a 20% discount on medicines, medical and dental services, diagnostic and laboratory fees, and professional fees in private hospitals and clinics. They also receive free healthcare services in government hospitals and continued benefits from GSIS, SSS, and PAG-IBIG. Additionally, express lanes in medical facilities ensure faster transactions for PWDs, improving their access to essential healthcare services.


Location:



Requirements:
1. Filipino Citizen
2. 59 years old and below
3. 1 x 1 ID pictures (2 pieces)
4. Barangay Clearance
5. Filled-up application form with City Health Doctor’s disability identification
6. Medical abstract from attending physician


Steps:
1. Secure PWD Application Form for Interview and Requirements A. Interview: with the client/any mentor of the family/Home visit if needed
2. Client proceed to the City Health Office for physician examination and identification of disabilities
3. Client to bring back the filled-up form for Persons with Disability ID
4. Client/liaison to proceed to City Mayor’s Office for signature

PhilHealth


Description:
The Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth) provides a comprehensive range of benefits to its members, covering various healthcare services. Inpatient benefits include coverage for hospital stays, room and board, medicines, laboratory tests, and professional fees, with the specific case rate amount deducted from the hospital bill before discharge. Outpatient benefits cover services such as day surgeries, hemodialysis, radiotherapy, and blood transfusions, with hemodialysis reimbursed at ₱4,000 per session for both inpatient and outpatient settings. The Z Benefits package is designed for catastrophic illnesses requiring prolonged treatment, covering conditions like certain cancers and end-stage renal disease, such as up to ₱500,000 for standard-risk acute lymphocytic leukemia in children. The Konsultasyong Sulit at Tama (Konsulta) Package provides primary care services, including consultations, health screenings, and assessments, promoting preventive care and early disease detection. The Outpatient Emergency Care Benefit (OECB) offers comprehensive coverage for emergency services, focusing on facility-based emergency care and pre-hospital services for cases that do not require hospital admission. Additionally, Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation Services cover rehabilitation programs and assistive mobility devices to aid in recovery and improve the quality of life. These benefits are available to all eligible PhilHealth members through accredited healthcare facilities across the Philippines.


Location:



Requirements for Membership:
1. PhilHealth Member Registration Form (PMRF): Download and fill out the form from the PhilHealth website.
2. Proof of Identity: Valid government-issued IDs such as a passport, driver's license, or birth certificate.
3. Additional Documents:
  a. Self-Employed or Voluntary Members: Proof of income like an Income Tax Return or a notarized affidavit of income declaration.
  b. OFWs: Proof of overseas employment such as a valid Overseas Employment Certificate.
  c. Indigent Members: Certificate of indigency from the DSWD.
  d. Senior Citizens: Senior Citizen ID or any valid ID showing the date of birth


Steps:
1. Ensure Active Membership: Confirm that your PhilHealth membership is active and that you have paid the required premium contributions. For most members, this means having at least nine months' worth of contributions within the 12 months prior to the first day of confinement.
2. Seek Care at Accredited Facilities: Receive medical services from PhilHealth-accredited hospitals or clinics and healthcare professionals to ensure coverage eligibility.
3. Prepare Necessary Documents: Gather and submit the following upon admission or before the procedure:
  PhilHealth Identification Number (PIN): Your unique member number.   Completed PhilHealth Claim Form 1: Available at hospitals or downloadable from the PhilHealth website.   Proof of Premium Payments: Official receipts or other valid proof, especially for voluntary or self-employed members.   Supporting Documents: Additional documents as required, such as a copy of your Member Data Record (MDR) or valid ID.
4. Coordinate with the Healthcare Provider: Inform the billing or admissions office of your intent to use PhilHealth benefits. They will assist in processing your documents and deducting the applicable benefits from your medical expenses.
5. Sign and Submit Forms: Before discharge, ensure all forms are accurately filled out and signed. The healthcare provider will then submit these to PhilHealth on your behalf.
For specific benefit packages, such as the Z Benefits for catastrophic illnesses, a pre-authorization from PhilHealth may be required. In such cases, the hospital will guide you through the necessary procedures.
For detailed information and updates, refer to the official PhilHealth website or consult directly with accredited healthcare providers.

Malasakit


Description:
The Malasakit Centers Act (Republic Act No. 11463) establishes Malasakit Centers as one-stop shops in all Department of Health (DOH) hospitals and the Philippine General Hospital (PGH) across the Philippines. These centers aim to streamline the process of availing medical and financial assistance by housing representatives from key government agencies, including the DOH, Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth), and Philippine Charity Sweepstakes Office (PCSO), in a single location. This setup ensures that indigent and financially incapacitated patients can efficiently access the support they need without the burden of navigating multiple offices.

The primary objective of Malasakit Centers is to reduce out-of-pocket expenses for patients by providing comprehensive assistance for medical costs not covered by existing health benefits. By consolidating services, these centers embody the Filipino value of "malasakit," or compassion, ensuring that healthcare services are accessible, efficient, and patient-centered.

Blood Transfusion


Description:
The Philippine Red Cross (PRC) provides essential blood services through its National Blood Service, ensuring a steady supply of safe and quality blood for patients in need. Individuals requiring blood can request it from any PRC Blood Service Facility by presenting a Blood Request Form issued by their attending physician or hospital, detailing the patient's information, diagnosis, blood type, and the required number of units. PRC also conducts voluntary blood donation drives to maintain an adequate blood supply and support patients undergoing surgeries, treatments, or emergencies. Additionally, PRC offers free or subsidized blood units for indigent patients through its Blood Samaritan Program, which helps cover processing fees. These services ensure that blood is readily available and accessible to those who need it most.


Location:



Requirements:
1. Blood request form


Steps:
1. Secure a Blood Request Form: Obtain this from your attending physician or hospital. Ensure it includes:
  a. Patient's full name
  b. Age and gender
  c. Citizenship
  d. Diagnosis
  e. Required blood type (ABO and Rh factor)
  f. Number of blood units/components needed
  g. Requesting physician's signature
  h. Hospital name
2. Visit a PRC Blood Service Facility: Bring the completed Blood Request Form to the nearest PRC facility. The PRC has streamlined its process, eliminating the need for a donor's card and waiving processing fees to expedite blood provision.
